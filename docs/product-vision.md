# Product Vision

## Vision

Alliance Command Center is the leadership platform for Last War alliances.

Our vision is to become the trusted source of truth for alliance leadership by transforming scattered participation data, leadership observations, and historical records into actionable insights that help leaders make fair, consistent, and objective decisions.

---

# Mission

Alliance leaders spend countless hours managing spreadsheets, screenshots, Discord messages, and personal notes to understand who contributes, who can be trusted, and who deserves additional responsibility.

Alliance Command Center centralizes this information into a single platform, allowing leadership teams to spend less time collecting data and more time leading their alliance.

---

# The Problem

Alliance leadership is fundamentally a people-management problem.

Today's leaders often struggle to answer questions such as:

* Who consistently participates?
* Who follows leadership direction?
* Who deserves promotion?
* Who should receive additional responsibility?
* Who has improved over the last season?
* Who is becoming less active?

The answers typically exist across:

* Google Sheets
* Discord channels
* Screenshots
* Personal notes
* Memory

As alliances grow, this information becomes increasingly difficult to manage.

Leadership discussions become subjective because historical information is fragmented or unavailable.

---

# Our Solution

Alliance Command Center provides a centralized leadership platform that combines:

* Member profiles
* Participation tracking
* Leadership observations
* Configurable evaluation metrics
* Historical data
* Long-term trends

The result is a shared source of truth that enables leadership teams to make informed decisions backed by objective data.

---

# Core Principles

## Historical Data Matters

Leadership decisions should be based on patterns over time rather than isolated events.

Historical information is one of the platform's greatest assets.

---

## Every Alliance Is Different

No two alliances value the same behaviors.

Some prioritize battle participation.

Others prioritize communication.

Others prioritize community.

Alliance Command Center should adapt to each alliance rather than forcing a single leadership philosophy.

---

## Leadership Is More Than Numbers

Participation metrics tell only part of the story.

Leadership also requires qualitative observations.

Examples include:

* Communication
* Reliability
* Initiative
* Leadership potential
* Teamwork

Objective metrics and leadership observations together provide a more complete picture of each member.

---

# Target Customers

Primary users include:

* Alliance Leaders (R5)
* Senior Leadership (R4)
* Recruiters
* Diplomats
* Battle Coordinators

These users are responsible for managing alliance operations and making leadership decisions.

---

# MVP

The initial release focuses on solving the most frequently validated customer problems.

Core capabilities include:

* Alliance management
* Member management
* Configurable metrics
* Historical metric tracking
* Leadership notes
* Weighted member evaluation
* Member rankings
* Historical member timelines

These features establish the foundation for data-driven leadership.

---

# Future Vision

As the platform evolves, Alliance Command Center will expand beyond manual data tracking.

Potential future capabilities include:

* Multi-language support
* OCR and screenshot imports
* Discord integration
* Automated participation tracking
* AI-assisted leadership insights
* Historical trend analysis
* Alliance family management
* Cross-alliance analytics

Future enhancements should build upon the core leadership platform without compromising its simplicity.

---

# Success Metrics

The product is successful when alliance leaders:

* Spend less time maintaining spreadsheets.
* Rely less on memory during leadership discussions.
* Make promotion decisions using historical data.
* Quickly identify reliable members.
* Trust Alliance Command Center as their primary leadership tool.

---

# Non-Goals

Alliance Command Center is not intended to:

* Replace the Last War game.
* Simulate gameplay.
* Provide cheats or automation.
* Become a social platform.
* Track every possible game statistic.

The platform exists to support alliance leadership, not replace the game experience.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Alliance Command Center aims to become the operating system for alliance leadership.

It should preserve institutional knowledge, improve collaboration across leadership teams, reduce subjectivity in decision-making, and help alliances build stronger communities through better leadership.
